Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between architectural and real estate photography?

Real estate photography is optimized for quick listing turnaround — efficient coverage to sell a property. Architectural photography is more deliberate — designed to document design intent, showcase craftsmanship, and create portfolio-quality images for architects and builders.

How do you handle Arizona's harsh sunlight?

Timing is everything. I schedule exterior shoots for golden hour or blue hour when possible. For midday shoots, I use techniques to manage contrast and harsh shadows. Interior work is less dependent on time of day.

Do you photograph buildings under construction?

Yes — construction progress documentation is a core service. I can set up recurring visits to document the build process, which is valuable for marketing, investor updates, and project records.

Can you photograph at night?

Absolutely. Twilight and night photography can be incredibly dramatic for the right building. I use professional lighting and long exposures to capture architecture after dark.

How far in advance should I book?

For optimal scheduling (best light conditions, no conflicts), 2-3 weeks is ideal. I can often accommodate shorter notice, but timing flexibility may be limited.
